    General Information
  • Scams are more common than most would think.
  • The transfer of currency has become increasingly more convenient due to new innovation in technology. However, with connectivity and convenience, comes people who seek to take advantage.

    • Who do scams affect the most?
  • Most people who fall into scams are the elderly who are not as knowledgeable in regards to technology.
  • Did you know that, according to FBI.gov, $1.4 billion was lost due to fraudulent activity in 2017 Facebook, MyHeritage and Under Armor have all had data breaches, revealing the identities and personal information over almost 300 million people

    • How do scammers acquire the actual money?
  • Mostly wire-transfer, credit card, bitcoin (as of recently)
  • Advance fee fraud - fake fees More than 800,000 dollars were lost to false billings False billing is the largest scam affecting individuals directly. As opposed to phishing, which relies on stealing personal data. Then later, used for financial gain. More Information: Source
  • Lottery, sweepstakes and competition scams More than 300,000 dollars were lost Has a similar principal to fee fraud in that this scam steals directly from the victim by manipulation. Giving them false hope of gaining financial wealth.
